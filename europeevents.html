31 Oct 2025

Delegation from the Saudi Arabian Ministry of Municipal Affairs and Housing visits ANCI Lombardia

Milan · 14:30–16:00

During the meeting, Maurizio Cabras, Coordinator of ANCI Lombardia's Department of Territory, Urban Planning, Public Works, and Construction, presented an update on the progress of public works in Lombardy, outlining the evolution of the market in recent years, from the pandemic emergency to the implementation of the National Recovery and Resilience Plan (PNRR).
The discussion with the Saudi delegation then focused on the issues of infrastructure management and maintenance, public-private partnerships, and the use of digital technologies to make cities more efficient and sustainable.

29 Oct 2025

Lithuanian delegation visits ANCI Lombardia

Milan · 14:30–16:00

A meeting was held at the ANCI Lombardy headquarters with a Lithuanian delegation, dedicated to discussing policies for employment, social inclusion, and work integration experiences promoted at the local level.
The role of Lombardy, recognized for its dynamic and innovative approach, was explored in depth. The Lombardy Region's policies in support of individuals and families were presented, along with some good practices and local projects to promote the independence of the most vulnerable people and their integration into the workplace.
At the end of the morning, the delegation had the opportunity to learn about the InLav Lombardia project, its objectives, aims, and expected results in terms of combating irregular work and promoting quality employment.

27 Oct 2025

Cohesion policies, European funds, and territorial development

Milan · 10:30–13:00

The event focused on assessing the implementation of the National Recovery and Resilience Plan (PNRR) and discussing future European cohesion policies. Speakers highlighted the key role of municipalities in delivering strategic projects and stressed the need for stronger territorial involvement in EU funding and policymaking for the 2028–2034 programming period.
Italian Minister Tommaso Foti reaffirmed support for administrative simplification and territorial management of cohesion funds, while regional and European representatives emphasized multilevel governance and cooperation. Discussions also addressed major local challenges such as housing, public services, and water management, alongside the importance of innovation, internationalization, and partnerships to strengthen regional competitiveness and sustainable development.
